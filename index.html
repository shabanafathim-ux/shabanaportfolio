Case Study — AI Governance for Clinical AI

AI Governance for Clinical Diagnostic Screening

HEALTHCARE · ISO/IEC 42001 · TRIAL DEPLOYMENT
Problem
Two AI diagnostic platforms (Diabetic Retinopathy & Cervical Cancer Screening) entered trial deployment within a public healthcare system with no existing governance precedent and clinicians unfamiliar with AI-assisted diagnostics.
AI Use Case
Machine learning models trained on clinical imaging data — including annotated pap smear slides — generating diagnostic predictions to support frontline clinical decision-making.
Governance Risks
Trust deficit among clinicians; vendor AI capability gaps (platforms built by external startups); absence of regulatory precedent for AI in this healthcare context.
Controls Applied
ISO/IEC 42001-aligned AI Management System covering model risk controls, AI vendor due diligence, and end-to-end audit-trail documentation across the trial lifecycle.
Human Oversight
Defined clinician-in-the-loop protocols ensuring AI outputs supported, never replaced, diagnostic judgement — with escalation paths for disputed model outputs.
Bias & Fairness
Structured bias evaluation checkpoints testing model outputs across patient demographics before and during trial deployment.
Performance Monitoring
Sensitivity and specificity metrics tracked and translated into clinically actionable explainability standards for frontline staff.
Audit Documentation
Model risk checklists and decision logs maintained throughout, established as repeatable artifacts for future AI procurements.
Outcome
Both platforms progressed through trial deployment with governance built into the design from day one. The model risk checklists and vendor assessment templates were adopted council-wide as the standard for all future AI procurements.

Case Study — Public Sector Digital Transformation

State-Wide Consumer Billing Platform — Kerala Water Authority

PMO · PROCUREMENT GOVERNANCE · PILOT-TO-ROLLOUT
Objective
Digitise a fragmented manual billing system causing multi-day delays in bill realisation for a state water utility.
Stakeholders
Kerala Water Authority leadership (including the Managing Director), technology startups, hardware vendors, and the council's own PMO function.
Procurement Approach
Structured EOI/RFP/QCBS-based procurement to select a delivery partner with the right technical capability.
Vendor Evaluation
When the originally selected startup proved technically unable to deliver, identified an alternative vendor and proposed an unpaid Proof of Concept to de-risk budget commitment before formal contracting.
Risk Management
Maintained structured RAID logs tracking delivery risk, vendor capability risk, and adoption risk throughout the program.
Milestone Governance
PRINCE2 Agile-structured milestone tracking from pilot through statewide rollout, with formal acceptance gates at each phase.
Reporting Structure
Power BI executive dashboards and direct steering committee reporting to senior Kerala Water Authority leadership.
Outcome
Bill realisation time reduced from 7 days to 3 days — a 57% improvement — with the platform progressing from pilot to full statewide rollout. The replacement vendor's strong performance earned them a follow-on contract with the Kerala State Electricity Board.
